Fishing License Requirements In Florida: Everything You Need To Know

Florida is one of the top fishing destinations in the United States, offering diverse opportunities for both freshwater and saltwater anglers. Whether you're planning a fishing trip with Matlacha Saltwater Outfitters or heading out on your own, understanding Florida's fishing license requirements is essential to ensure compliance with state regulations. The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC) oversees the licensing process, ensuring that the state's rich marine and freshwater ecosystems remain sustainable for future generations. Below, we’ll break down the different types of fishing licenses, exemptions, and how you can obtain one before your next fishing adventure.

Who Needs a Florida Fishing License?

In Florida, most residents and visitors are required to have a valid fishing license when fishing in both freshwater and saltwater. This rule applies whether you are fishing from a boat, pier, shoreline, or any other location. Licenses are categorized into freshwater and saltwater, so depending on your target species, you may need one or both. However, some anglers are exempt from needing a fishing license. The following individuals do not require a Florida fishing license:

  • Florida residents 65 years or older with proof of age and residency.
  • Florida residents under 16 years old.
  • Individuals fishing from a licensed pier.
  • Florida residents fishing in their county of residence using a cane pole or handline (non-commercial purposes only).
  • Active-duty military personnel home on leave.
  • Individuals fishing on charter boats or with a licensed fishing guide, such as Matlacha Saltwater Outfitters, as the guide's license covers all passengers.
  • Florida residents receiving food assistance, disability benefits, or other eligible government aid (with proper documentation).

Types of Fishing Licenses in Florida

Florida offers a variety of fishing licenses to accommodate different angling needs. Below are the most common types:

  • Freshwater Fishing License – Required for anyone targeting freshwater species such as largemouth bass, crappie, and bluegill.
  • Saltwater Fishing License – Required for anglers fishing in saltwater and targeting species like snook, redfish, and tarpon.
  • Combination License – Available for Florida residents who want both freshwater and saltwater fishing privileges.
  • Sportsman’s and Lifetime Licenses – For avid anglers, Florida offers sportsman’s licenses that include hunting and fishing privileges, as well as lifetime licenses for long-term savings.
  • Shoreline Fishing License (Residents Only) – Florida residents who fish only from the shore or a structure attached to the shore can obtain a no-cost shoreline fishing license.

Some specific fish species, such as snook and spiny lobster, require additional permits that can be added to your license for an extra fee.

How to Get a Florida Fishing License

Obtaining a fishing license in Florida is simple and can be done through multiple channels:

  • Online at the official FWC website.
  • In-person at approved retailers such as Walmart, bait and tackle shops, or sporting goods stores.
  • By phone through the FWC’s licensing hotline.
  • At local tax collector’s offices across Florida.

Prices vary depending on residency status, license type, and duration. Non-residents typically pay higher fees compared to Florida residents.

Florida Fishing Regulations and Bag Limits

Holding a valid fishing license doesn’t mean you can keep unlimited amounts of fish. Florida has strict bag limits, size restrictions, and seasonal closures for many species to protect fish populations. Before heading out, check the latest FWC regulations for popular game fish such as snook, redfish, and tarpon.
